Chapter 21: Global Futures

Imagining Tomorrow's World

Envisioning the Future

The future of global politics is not pre-ordained. It is an open-ended story that will be shaped by the interplay of powerful structural forces and the choices made by individuals, states, and organizations. By examining key trends, we can imagine a range of possible futures.

A Borderless World

The hyperglobalist vision. In this future, economic globalization triumphs, leading to the erosion of state borders and the emergence of a single global market. The nation-state becomes little more than a "transmission belt" for global capitalism.

A World of Democracies

The liberal vision of an "end of history." In this future, liberal democracy becomes the universal form of government, leading to a "democratic peace" and a world characterized by cooperation and integration.

A Clash of Civilizations

The Huntingtonian vision. In this future, cultural and religious identity becomes the primary source of conflict, leading to a world of tension and rivalry between major civilizational blocs.

A Chinese Century?

The power transition vision. In this future, the decline of US hegemony and the spectacular rise of China leads to a new bipolar or unipolar world order centered on Beijing. This could be a peaceful transition or a period of intense great-power conflict.

Growth of International Community

The cosmopolitan vision. In this future, growing interdependence and awareness of shared global problems (like climate change) foster a sense of global citizenship and strengthen the institutions of global governance, leading to a more peaceful and just world.

Key Drivers of the Future

Several powerful trends are likely to shape which of these futures comes to pass.

Shifting Power Balances

The diffusion of power away from the West and towards emerging economies in the East and South is the defining power trend of our time. How this "power shift" is managed will be critical for global stability.

The Crisis of Capitalism

The 2008 financial crisis shattered confidence in the neoliberal model of market fundamentalism. The future of the global economy—and the search for a more stable and equitable alternative—remains a central challenge.

The Ecological Crisis

Climate change, resource scarcity, and biodiversity loss are existential threats that will increasingly dominate the global agenda, forcing states to cooperate in unprecedented ways or face catastrophic consequences.

The Unknowable Future

The only certainty about the future is its uncertainty. History is not a straight line; it is full of surprises, crises, and turning points. The task of the student of global politics is not to predict the future, but to understand the forces that are shaping it, and the choices that lie before us.
