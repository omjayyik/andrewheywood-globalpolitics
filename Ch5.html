Chapter 5: The State & Foreign Policy

Has Globalization Changed Everything?

The Fate of the State

For centuries, the state has been the dominant actor on the world stage. But has globalization made the state obsolete? This question sparks one of the most intense debates in global politics.

Thesis: The Retreating State

This view argues that globalization is hollowing out the power of the state from above, below, and sideways. States are losing control over their economies, cultures, and borders.

Antithesis: The Transformed State

This view counters that while the role of the state is changing, it remains the most powerful actor. States are not victims of globalization; they are its principal architects and continue to adapt and thrive.

The Billiard Ball Model (Realist View)

This model sees states as unitary, rational actors. Foreign policy is simply a calculated response to external threats and opportunities in the anarchic international system. Domestic politics are irrelevant.

The Bureaucratic Politics Model

This model opens up the "black box" of the state. It argues that foreign policy is not a rational choice, but the result of bargaining and compromise among different government agencies and departments, each with its own interests ("where you stand depends on where you sit").

The Cognitive Process Model

This model focuses on the psychology of individual leaders. It argues that foreign policy decisions are shaped by the perceptions, beliefs, and misperceptions of key decision-makers, who operate in a world of imperfect information.

What is Power?

Power is the central concept in global politics, but its meaning is highly contested. Has globalization changed the nature of power itself?

Hard Power

The ability to get what you want through coercion or payment. This is the traditional view of power, based on military might ("sticks") and economic strength ("carrots").

Soft Power

The ability to get what you want through attraction and persuasion rather than force. This is based on a country's culture, political ideals, and policies. In a globalized world, soft power is becoming increasingly important.
